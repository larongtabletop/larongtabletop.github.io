Patricia Ramos

A part-time teacher, a freelance illustrator, and tells stories using words and pictures. She is a graduate of the UP College of Fine Arts.

Juan Miguel Ala-Tolentino

A study coach under Project POND (People Overcoming Neurodevelopmental Difficulties). Graduated from BA Journalism in the University of the Philippines Diliman. 

He is currently a freelance writer and editor for various projects. He is the co-designer for upcoming tabletop games Tala and Talinghaga.

According to Patricia, Talinghaga started as her thesis as an undergraduate that wanted to showcase or teach something Filipino. Adding along with her interest in Baybayin and wanting to learn it for herself thought it would be a perfect opportunity for her to focus on. Having both her and Juan jotting down the first set of rules on the back of a ministop receipt.

With the aftermath of Talinghaga, Tala came in materialized from a bunch of random prototypes. Inspired from the game ‘Counter-Strike’, it evolved from it’s first version named ‘Burda’ with the “place-a-token-take-a-token” system, then to ‘Tala’ when the creator noticed the tiles looked like the sky, with clouds where you can’t place tokens (symbolized as a star), adding the theme of stars and Philippine Mythology with the help of Patricia Ramos with the visualizations.


  • Patricia: It was — for me personally it was having to fit it in the timeline. Having to fit it into time constraints, having to rack your brain about math and analysis like: “How do I make this a fun and balanced game at the same time?” I still had to read more about board games in general and I am not a math person either so parang “Why did I do this to myself?” (laughs) so yun, basically that was the hardest part for me.


  • Juan: Well sakin, talaga with Talinghaga and Tala, and a couple of other games I am working on, it’s fun eh. I like the challenge.



  • Patricia: Go for it (laughs)



  • Juan: Go for it and have fun, just have fun


  • Patricia: Have an open mind, don’t be afraid to put your own game out there. If you’re scared of your idea being stolen, for me there is no such originality anyways, it’s only the execution that’s going to make you unique.

Thoughts on Darna and Talinghaga

Talinghaga

Talinghaga is a Filipino board game which can be described as Filipino Scrabble. The game consists of cards containing syllables/letters from the Filipino alphabet with their corresponding, written in both phonetic and alibata. The game’s rules were easy enough to understand as well as the point system. It’s a fun board game to play, and I think it is a good exercise and test to see how well you know the Filipino language. One of the interesting features of the game is how you can steal an opponent’s created word and make it into a much longer word to claim your own. I hope this game gets developed more for mass production, as it is a great way for teaching someone the Filipino language as well. Its suitable for learning children to adults who barely use the language. You can play it with your friends who know Filipino very well and have a battle of who knows more words or with your friends who don’t know how to speak it very well and make fun of them, teaching them in the process. It’s a fun game for everyone.

 

Darna Card Game

The game’s rules are simple, shuffled bato cards would be given to you, not knowing if what you get is THE bato or not. Along with the bato card, you get to discard cards that would either help or hinder you into winning. The goal is for you to get the crystal bato card, and if you THINK or if you are sure you have it, you shout “DARNA”. If you do have it, you win and if you don’t you’re out. It’s a fun game to play with friends and also a unique and challenging strategy game. It can also be quite thrilling as you’re not sure who has the actual bato, and also hilarious to see which one shouts darna. One of the things that appealed to me was the art on the cards. It was interesting and fun, as each card has a character and corresponding move.

 

Gabrielle Dimalanta
